Sir Tim Berners-Lee

The creator of the World Wide Web

Tim Berners-Lee is also the founder and current director of the The World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), the World Wide Web's main international standards organization.

Here's a timeline of Tim Berners-Lee's life:

  • 1955 - Born while both of his parents were working on the first commercially-built computer, the Ferranti Mark I.
  • 1969 - Studied at London's independent Emanuel School.
  • 1973 - Enrolled at the Queen’s College of the University of Oxford and graduated with a first-class degree in physics.
  • 1976 - appointed as an engineer at the telecommunications company, Plessey in Poole, working on distributed transaction systems, message relays, and bar code technology.
  • 1978 - Joined D. G. Nash Ltd, working on typesetting software for intelligent printers and a multitasking operating system.
  • 1980 - Worked as a consultant sofware engineer at CERN, creating a program called "Enquire", a simple hyperlink program, laying the foundations for the World Wide Web.
  • 1981 - started working at John Poole’s Image Computer Systems, Ltd where he worked on real time control firmware, graphics and communications software, and a generic macro language.
  • 1984 - Received a fellowship at CERN and continued working there, realising that there was a need for a simpler and more efficient method of data sharing.
  • 1989 - conceptualised and proposed the World Wide Web, an information sharing system that could be implemented around the world.
  • 1991 - Made the world's first ever website, "info.cern.ch" which provided information on what the World Wide Web was and how it could be used for sharing information.
  • 1994 - Founded the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C) deciding that it's technologies should be royalty-free so anyone could benefit from them.
  • 1995 - presented with The Software System Award from the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM).
  • 1999 - named as one of the 100 Most Important People of the 20th century by the Time Magazine.
  • 2004 - Became a professor of Computer Science at the university of Southampton, where he worked on the Semantic Web.
  • 2006 - Became the co-director of the Web Science Trust, which was launched to optimise its usage and design.
  • 2009 - Started serving as the director of the World Wide Web Foundation.
  • 2013 - Became one of five Internet and Web pioneers awarded the inaugural Queen Elizabeth Prize for Engineering.

"Anyone who has lost track of time when using a computer knows the propensity to dream, the urge to make dreams come true and the tendency to miss lunch."

-Tim Berners-Lee
